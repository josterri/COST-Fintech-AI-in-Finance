Comprehensive Progress Report at 24 Months (September 2020 - September 2022)

Mid-Term Report / Action Chair Report

Executive Summary

The COST Action on Fintech and AI in Finance has brought together an incredibly diverse network and we have witnessed an enormous growth of our Action to 260 interdisciplinary researchers from 49 countries, with 39 of those countries being European COST countries, to become one of the largest and most active COST Actions in Europe.

The academic achievements of the network are impressive. The COST Action has organized 150 research conferences with more than 6,000 participants and 300 distinguished speakers. The network's publications have been cited more than 10,000 times, demonstrating the impact and relevance of the research being conducted within the network.

MoU Objectives - Detailed Progress

76-100% Complete

Objective 1: Blended Approaches for Financial Services Evaluation

Developing blended approaches to evaluate innovative financial services and their providers, especially in the FinTech domain, building on Machine Learning methods, focusing on prediction (early warning) of operational fragility, fraudulent and illegal behavior.

Key Achievements:

  • Strategic partnership with Romanian Fintech company iFactor for AI tools development
  • Partnership with ING Group Amsterdam for early warning credit systems
  • Swiss National Science Foundation project on Anomaly and Fraud Detection in Blockchain
  • Research project with Swiss Fintech company on default rating of loans
76-100% Complete

Objective 2: Conceptual Tools for Black-Box Model Transparency

Development of conceptual and methodological tools for establishing when black-box models are admissible and making them more transparent and/or replacing them with interpretable and explainable models.

Key Achievements:

  • Created extensive platform: explainableaiforfinance.com
  • Disseminated code and training material via quantlet.com and quantinar.com
  • Published research on interpretability algorithms applied to random forests
  • Introduced measures of local and global interpretability for ML tasks
51-75% Complete

Objective 3: Regulator and Practitioner Input

Receive input from regulators and practitioners' communities and validate results with regard to increasing transparency of artificial intelligence applications.

Key Achievements:

  • High-level COST policy event planned at COST Association Brussels (May 2023)
  • Member accepted onto CEDPO AI Working Group in Brussels
  • Action Chair joint appointment with University of Twente and ING Group
  • Close contacts with AI team from European Central Bank
26-50% Complete

Objective 4: Performance Attribution Models

Pruning and improvement of performance attribution models by contributing to methodologies for reducing false discovery rate in financial research and applied financial investment management.

Key Outputs:

  • SSRN Paper: Data-driven Case-based Reasoning in Bankruptcy Prediction
  • Credit reform dataset at blockchain-research-center.com (WG1 leader Prof. Hardle)
  • Goal: Consolidate research into unifying position papers in next grant periods
76-100% Complete

Objective 5: Dissemination to Public and Regulators

Disseminate to the public and share with regulators the results on investment product performance evaluation.

Key Achievements:

  • 150+ research events with 6,000+ participants from academia, industry, regulators
  • Meetup group with 2,000+ members: Fintech AI in Finance
  • Code dissemination via quantlet.com and quantinar.com
  • COST CA19130 meets Brussels event planned for GP3
51-75% Complete

Objective 6: European Platform for Investment Products (Modified)

Original: Creation of European platform comparing out-of-sample performance of banks' investment products.

Proposed Replacement: Creation of a stable, transparent European platform for research on the digitalisation of Banks' financial products, including digital assets, tokens in a reproducible framework.

Reason for change: Due to changing regulatory landscape in Europe, enhanced privacy, data protection and regulatory requirements, the original objective is no longer legally feasible.

76-100% Complete
