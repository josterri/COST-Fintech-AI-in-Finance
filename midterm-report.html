Executive Summary

The COST Action on Fintech and AI in Finance has brought together an incredibly diverse network, witnessing enormous growth to 260 interdisciplinary researchers from 49 countries, with 39 European COST countries, becoming one of the largest and most active COST Actions in Europe.

The academic achievements are impressive: the Action organized 150 research conferences with more than 6,000 participants and 300 distinguished speakers. The network's publications have been cited more than 10,000 times, demonstrating the impact and relevance of research conducted within the network.

Rapporteur Assessment

"The FinTech and AI in Finance Action has demonstrably been highly successful in nurturing the field, supporting the development of a strong community of data scientists, economic and social science experts, practitioners, and stakeholders including regulators and policy-makers from 49 countries."

The Action promotes diversity and collaboration through STSMs, virtual grants, and initiatives encouraging researchers from different backgrounds to work together. It bridges the gap between practitioners from the finance industry, academics, and regulators through common knowledge exchange platforms.

Rapporteur Assessment

"The FinTech and AI in Finance Action has demonstrably been highly successful in nurturing the field, and supporting the development of a strong community of data scientists, economic and social science experts and practitioners, and a wide array of stakeholders, including regulators and policy-makers from 49 countries. The programme has made impressive progress throughout its first 24 months, generating over 100 peer-reviewed publications, numerous cross-national collaborative initiatives, and supporting a sizeable cohort of early career and other academic researchers."

Key Highlights from Rapporteur Review:

  • Significant role in promoting state of the art in financial data science
  • Active support for Explainable and Interpretable AI development
  • Critical contribution to involving regulators and policy-makers in DeFi, cryptocurrencies, and decentralized assets
  • Important frameworks and platforms for hosting and sharing datasets
  • Impressive mobilization of a robust FinTech community
  • Successful dissemination of highly consequential research
"Overall, the COST FinTech and AI in Finance Action has so far made impressive headways in mobilising a robust FinTech community and progressing the State of the Art in FinTech-related data science and successfully disseminating a large body of associated and highly consequential research."

Overall Assessment: The level of ambition of MoU objectives is Medium. Most MoU Objectives are progressing appropriately. 15 out of 16 objectives confirmed by Rapporteur.
